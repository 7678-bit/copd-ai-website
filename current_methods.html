Overview

Current COPD monitoring practices involve periodic clinical visits, patient self-reporting, and reactive treatments. These methods are limited in predicting and preventing exacerbations.

Standard Monitoring Techniques

  • Spirometry: Measures lung function (FEV1/FVC) but is only available in clinical settings.
  • Pulse oximetry: Tracks blood oxygen levels but doesnâ€™t identify trends without long-term data.
  • Patient diaries: Subjective and often inaccurate or inconsistent.
  • Physician observation: Reactive rather than preventive.

Limitations

These methods are:

  • Not real-time
  • Lacking predictive capacity
  • Dependent on in-person visits or patient initiative

Need for Improvement

To reduce hospitalizations and improve outcomes, there is a need for continuous, remote, and predictive monitoring systems that provide actionable insights in real time.
