How COPD Affects the Body

COPD primarily affects the lungs but has systemic implications on the whole body. It damages the airways and alveoli (air sacs), reducing airflow and causing trapped air in the lungs.

Key Physiological Changes

  • Airway Obstruction: Inflammation narrows the bronchial tubes, making it hard to exhale.
  • Loss of Elasticity: Alveolar walls break down, reducing lung elasticity and gas exchange efficiency.
  • Mucus Overproduction: Excess mucus clogs airways, exacerbating breathing difficulties.

Systemic Effects

Over time, COPD leads to oxygen deprivation throughout the body, which can affect the heart, brain, and muscles. Many patients experience fatigue, cognitive decline, and complications like pulmonary hypertension or heart failure.

Why Early Detection Matters

Understanding the physiological changes helps clinicians recognize symptoms early. AI systems can analyze patterns in lung sounds or breathing to detect these changes before a patient experiences a crisis.
