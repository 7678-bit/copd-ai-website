Overview

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) is a long-term, progressive lung disease that makes breathing difficult. It encompasses conditions like emphysema and chronic bronchitis and is characterized by airflow limitation and inflammation in the lungs.

Causes and Risk Factors

  • Long-term exposure to tobacco smoke (primary cause)
  • Air pollution and occupational dusts
  • Genetic factors, such as alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency

Symptoms

  • Persistent cough with mucus
  • Shortness of breath, especially during physical activities
  • Wheezing and chest tightness
  • Frequent respiratory infections

Impact on Patients

COPD is a major cause of disability and is associated with increased hospital admissions and reduced quality of life. The disease requires continuous management and often leads to severe health complications if not addressed early.

As of 2025, COPD remains one of the top causes of death globally. However, modern technologies like AI offer promising new ways to detect and manage this condition more effectively.
