Understanding Exacerbations

A COPD exacerbation is a sudden worsening of symptoms such as breathlessness, coughing, and mucus production. These flare-ups are often triggered by infections, air pollution, or weather changes and may require hospitalization if not managed quickly.

Common Symptoms of COPD

  • Chronic cough with mucus
  • Shortness of breath (dyspnea)
  • Wheezing and chest tightness
  • Fatigue and reduced exercise capacity

Impact of Exacerbations

Frequent exacerbations can accelerate the decline in lung function and quality of life. They also increase the risk of mortality and healthcare costs.
